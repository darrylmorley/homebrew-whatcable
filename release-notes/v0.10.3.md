Italian and Armenian translations, i18n overhaul

## What's new

- **Italian translation.** Full coverage of the menu bar app and diagnostic engine. Thanks to [@bovirus](https://github.com/bovirus) for the translation (PR [#114](https://github.com/darrylmorley/whatcable/pull/114)).

- **Armenian translation.** Full coverage of the menu bar app UI. Thanks to [@Vardan933](https://github.com/Vardan933) for the translation (PR [#107](https://github.com/darrylmorley/whatcable/pull/107)).

- **i18n migrated from `.xcstrings` to `.lproj/.strings`.** The single-JSON-per-module format made contributor translations hard to diff and review. Each language now lives in its own `Localizable.strings` file, one per module, which is easier to edit and less likely to produce merge conflicts. Thanks to [@IonBazan](https://github.com/IonBazan) for driving this migration (PR [#118](https://github.com/darrylmorley/whatcable/pull/118)).

- **Live language switching in Settings.** The language picker now includes Italian and Armenian alongside English, Polish, and Simplified Chinese.

- **59 obsolete USB-IF vendor IDs added.** Vendors that USB-IF has marked as obsolete are now included in the bundled database. Cables from these vendors previously showed "Unregistered / unknown" and tripped a trust-signal flag; they now get their correct name.
