# Charger wattage fix, plus clearer Intel and updater messages

## Fixed

- **Charger wattage no longer sticks at 3W when a second device is plugged
  in.** Charging over MagSafe with a third-party adapter while also using a
  USB-C display (or anything else on a second port) could freeze the reading
  at "3W", even though the Mac was charging at full wattage. Unplugging the
  display used to be the only way to correct it. Thanks @hinaloe for the clear
  report and the diagnostics (#443).

- **Intel Macs now say why the app can't work.** WhatCable relies on data
  Intel Macs don't expose, so it can't report cables on them. Instead of an
  empty ports list, Intel users get a clear message explaining that (#407).

- **Power Monitor explains a connected-but-not-selected charger.** When a
  charger is plugged in but the Mac isn't drawing from it, the monitor used
  to spin forever (#296).

- **The window comes to the front in window mode** when reopened (#419).

- **Updater gives clearer guidance when the app bundle is missing.** If the
  running app was moved (for example, left in Downloads), the updater now
  explains what to do instead of failing with a cryptic error.

## Also in this release

- The consent sheet and privacy policy now describe exactly what the
  diagnostic probes send.
- Diagnostic probes are now bounded and can't run away, thanks @VailElla
  (#451), and the CLI sanitises text that comes from hardware before printing
  it, also @VailElla (#453).
- Translation updates: Traditional Chinese, thanks @jimmyorz (#433, #447),
  and Italian, thanks @bovirus (#448).
